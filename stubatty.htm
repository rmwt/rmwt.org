These classes are based on improving the understanding and application of the most effective way to cut wood on a lathe. It is for every level of woodturner from a complete beginner to professional, everyone has very similar problems.

There are six fundamental cuts in woodturning, almost identical in spindle and bowl turning. This style of woodturning is hundreds of years old and based on the European style of push cut, simplified to work with less tools and simple grinds.

Everyone who takes the class learns to cut the wood with zero torn grain and very little physical effort. Almost any cut can be performed with 2lbs of pressure or less.

Each cut is broken down in to very small parts, so that anyone can learn and perfect these cuts. The six cuts are practiced in a variety of both spindle and bowl turning shapes.

Practicing correctly is the key to learning well. Students have the use of an unlimited amount of wood to practice with, most use between 8 & 10 pieces per day, usually Silver Maple.

Each student learns to free hand grind, which is much more accurate than jig grinding, not something the manufacturers of the jigs wish for you to know.

Myths like "Rub the bevel" are dismissed, as no single cut can be performed without problems if any pressure is applied to the bevel. It must be floated and this is very clearly explained and taught throughout the class.

The first two days are based on improving and understanding technique; the third day onwards includes practicing whilst completing pieces.

This class has been taught over 27 years to over 3000 woodturners.

The wood is included in the price of the class, no matter how many pieces you turn.
